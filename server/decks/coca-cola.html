Brand Overview

Coca-Cola is not just a beverage, it's a cultural icon that has been a part of people's lives for over 125 years. From its humble beginnings at a soda fountain in downtown Atlanta in 1886, Coca-Cola has grown to become the world's best-selling soda, providing billions of moments of refreshment every day. The name Coca-Cola itself refers to two of its original ingredients, coca leaves, and kola nuts, showcasing the brand's rich history and heritage. Throughout the years, Coca-Cola has been at the forefront of innovation and advertising. From the iconic Spencerian script logo to the introduction of the contour bottle designed to be recognized by feel in the dark, Coca-Cola has always been a pioneer in the industry. Did you know that if all the Coca-Cola ever produced were distributed to each person in the world, there would be 1,104 bottles per person? That's the kind of impact this brand has had on a global scale. Coca-Cola's presence in popular culture is unmistakable, from the famous "I'd Like to Buy the World a Coke" commercial in 1971 to the heartwarming "Mean" Joe Greene ad in 1979. The brand's sponsorship of major sporting events like the Olympic Games and FIFA World Cup has solidified its position as the world's biggest sports fan. As Coca-Cola celebrates its 125th anniversary, it is clear that this iconic brand is not just a soda, it's a symbol of joy, togetherness, and refreshment. With innovations like the Coca-Cola Freestyle fountain dispenser and sustainable initiatives like the PlantBottle PET packaging, Coca-Cola is committed to creating a better future while continuing to bring happiness to people around the world. Cheers to 125 years of Coca-Cola, and here's to many more years of refreshing moments to come.

Global Sponsorships

Coca-Cola is one of the world's biggest sports fans, with the ability to sponsor the two biggest sporting events, the Olympics and FIFA World Cup, for over 80 years. The company's long-standing sponsorships include providing drinks at the 1928 Olympic Games in Amsterdam and forging the first-ever sponsorship between a company and an international sports governing body with FIFA in 1976. Coca-Cola has been a sponsor not only of the FIFA World Cup but also other FIFA tournaments, showcasing its commitment to sports on a global scale.

Innovations in Packaging

The Coca-Cola Company continues to innovate in packaging with initiatives like the PlantBottle PET packaging, partially made from plant-based materials and fully recyclable. The introduction of the two-liter contour packaging and the Simply billion-dollar brand reflect the company's commitment to sustainability and meeting consumer needs. Innovations like the Coca-Cola Freestyle fountain dispenser, offering over 100 beverage choices, demonstrate the company's dedication to providing diverse and eco-friendly packaging options for consumers.
